Hi my name is Shinya! I'm a CFD Engineer specializing in understanding airflow in-and-around the urban built environment. My main professional interests are in turbulence physics, urban microclimate, and HVAC design. I am currently an Air Modeling Specialist at Arcadis.

<!-- ![](/media/profile.jpg) -->

Prior to joining the Design Engineering Industry, I worked as an engineering researcher where my work focused on identifying building blocks of fluid flows to create reduced-order models of complex flow phenomena. 
As a Ph.D. candidate in Dr. George Ilhwan Park's [Computational Fluids Group](https://park.seas.upenn.edu) at the Uniersity of Pennsylvania where I worked on reduced-order modeling of near wall turbulnce using data-driven and operator-driven techniques. During my undergraduate studies at Columbia University, I was an undergraduate researcher in Dr. Marco Giometto's [Environmental Flow Physics Lab](https://efpl.engineering.columbia.edu)  where I worked on an analytical formulation of the wake formation behind a wind turbine. 

I received my M.S. in Mechanical Engineering from the Univeristy of Pennsylvania in 2022 and B.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Columbia University in 2020.

Outside of research, I enjoy playing the piano, swimming, and crafting.
